ICYF Young Volunteers Camp[Turkey]
ABOUT

ICYF Young Volunteers Camp 2023 is an opportunity for young and emerging leaders to learn advanced methods of countering the rising social and humanitarian crises. The 5th ICYF International Young Volunteers Camp opens the avenues for promising and enthusiastic youth to participate in different projects while being part of this camp and broadens their vision about how to overcome a massive level of problems in different circumstances. In this funded volunteer camp in Turkey, the participants acutely learn to act actively in times of natural disasters and humanitarian crises. ICYF Youth Camp 2022 is organized by the Islamic Cooperation Youth Forum with the collaboration of the Ministry of Youth and Sports of the Republic of Türkiye. The distinctive feature of this funded Volunteering Opportunity in Antalya is that there are separate days of volunteering activities for males and for females. Each group can find its own strengths and weaknesses in responding to these natural disasters and humanitarian crises. Moreover, the processes which are being followed in this internationally funded volunteer camp for young leaders are of a theoretical as well as practical nature and include simulative sessions and workshops.

The International Volunteer forum for Young and emerging leaders shows the interest of many people to participate in these activities for multiple reasons such as to eradicate poverty, improve basic health conditions, empower people with education and employment, provide people with safe drinking water, cope with environmental problems and so many other reasons that are imperative to bring a positive change in the society. In this ICYF Volunteer opportunity, there are many tasks that are being designed to equip the young leader with the skills that lead to growth and sustainable development.

The ICYF 2023 Volunteer Camp has a distinctive purpose for this very specific year which is to raise awareness among the youth of the world regarding the new emerging humanitarian crises and challenges within the OIC geography. Furthermore, the young leaders through practical work and interactive skills will be prepared for emergencies in times of natural disasters and other environmental crises. From a broader perspective, all this character development of the youth aims at leading them toward maintaining global peace and sustainable human development. Thus, this funded ICYF Volunteer Camp is actually psycho-social support for the participants to learn from the experts about how to react in times of crisis.

Requirments



📌The participants can be from OIC member states. They can also be from the OIC observatory state or representatives of a Muslim community in a non-OIC member state.

📌The participants must be able to communicate in English.

📌Only individuals between 18–35 years of age are eligible.

📌The participants must have a distinct experience in humanitarian and volunteer work.

📌The participants must be able to provide proof of the volunteer work.

📌The participants must be able to attend the full camp training program.

Benefits



The local transportation cost will be covered by the organization.

Food and accommodation will be covered by the organization.

The participants will be given a city tour at the expense of the host organization.

The organization will send invitation letters to the participants to help them in their visa process
