How to Survive College?

College is an exciting and transformative journey that presents numerous opportunities and challenges. It’s crucial to approach college life with a well-rounded strategy encompassing academics, social engagement, career exploration, and mental and physical health.

We’ll delve into these aspects of being a college student, providing you with practical tips and insights to survive and thrive during your college years.

With so many responsibilities to juggle, college can move at a fast pace for many college students. Worrying about paying for tuition and fees only adds to the stress of academic and social life. Luckily, applying for scholarships for college students can help reduce the financial burden of higher education, allowing you to apply your full attention to social and academic happenings.


What are needed?

Many college students live away from home for the first time in their lives and have complete control over their schedules and how they spend their time. As a result, first-year students may struggle to adequately manage their time and maintain a consistent routine that will allow them to succeed. Time management tools and strategies can help you balance the responsibilities of college life and stay on top of your academics.

Time Management and Organization

Tip 1: Utilize Digital Tools
In the digital age, it’s crucial to harness the power of technology, and being a college student is no exception. Take advantage of calendars and planners on your phone, tablet, or laptop to help you keep track of classes, upcoming due dates, exams, internship applications, and more!

Tip 2: Break It Down
One of college’s most common - and most detrimental - pitfalls is procrastination. With so many aspects of college life to manage, many first-year students can feel overwhelmed and resort to putting off their assignments or studying until the last minute. Unfortunately, doing so can result in poor academic performance and low grades, delaying your progress toward your degree.

Tip 3: Plan Your Studying Ahead of Time
It’s a good idea to set aside time during the first few weeks of the semester to plan your studying for major assignments and exams. Most professors provide a class syllabus in the first week of class, which details all of the exams, projects, and assignments for the entire semester. Utilize your course syllabi to your advantage and plan your study schedule for major exams so you’re not left cramming the night before.

Active Learning

College is more than just attending lectures and taking notes. It’s about actively engaging in your coursework, absorbing knowledge, and developing critical thinking skills. Active learning not only enhances your understanding of the subject matter but also helps you retain information more effectively.
